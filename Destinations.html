Tbilisi

Tbilisi is the capital and the largest city of Georgia, lying on the banks of the Kura River with a population of around 1.2 million people. Tbilisi was founded in the fifth century AD by Vakhtang I of Iberia, and since then has served as the capital of various Georgian kingdoms and republics. Between 1801 and 1917, then part of the Russian Empire, Tiflis was the seat of the Caucasus Viceroyalty, governing both the northern and the southern parts of the Caucasus.

North Georgia

Kazbegi is located in the north-east of Georgia, 150 kilometers from Tbilisi. Its administrative center is Stepantsminda (1750 meters above sea level).
.It is distinguished by the variety of nature and unique architecture. The villages of Mutso and Shatili located here are included in the list of UNESCO candidates. Ancient customs and ancient pagan religious rituals are best preserved in Khevsureti, which local residents do not forget. Khevsuri cuisine and the unique method of brewing beer are worthy of particular mention.
.Svaneti is one of the most popular and growing tourist attractions in Georgia. The variety of cultural and natural resources of Svaneti is a unique opportunity for the guests who come here to relax, taste delicious Svan dishes, enjoy the beautiful nature, learn about the rich culture, traditions and history of Svaneti.

West Georgia

Did you know that West Georgia is one of the best places in the world to watch migratory birds? More than a million birds fly to the Black Sea coast every year! 15 guides were trained in cooperation with USAID, Georgian Ecotourism Association and Adjara Tourism Department. They will help to increase the number of tourists watching migratory birds. Today USAID Mission Deputy Director David Hoffman handed the guides binoculars and telescopes. We congratulate the young guides on the start of their activities. We expect interesting tours from you.

South Georgia

Historical-geographic area Samtskhe-Javakheti is located in the southern part of Georgia, it is the administrative center of Akhaltsikhe region. It is in Akhaltsikhe that one of the most popular tourist attractions of Georgia is located - Rabati Castle: the history of the castle begins in the 9th century. It is from this time that the toponym "Akhaltsikhe" appears. In the late Middle Ages, Samtskhe-Javakheti was ruled by the Ottomans, which led to the spread and dominance of Eastern culture. In 2010-2012, the prison was fundamentally reconstructed and currently represents a synthesis of European and Asian culture.
